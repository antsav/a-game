# Starter Pack (first-purchase offer)

**What:** a one-time, high-value bundle shown early and once, to cross a player from
non-payer to payer.
**Monetization goal:** conversion rate (the first dollar — the highest-leverage point).
**Best for:** any hybrid-casual game with an IAP layer; the first IAP to build after
remove-ads.
**Psychology:** anchoring + scarcity + "the first purchase anchors all future spend" —
see [[references/conversion-funnel.md]], [[references/spend-psychology.md]].

## How it works
A bundle (premium currency + a booster + sometimes a rare cosmetic/unit) at **$0.99-4.99**,
shown with a high struck-through "value," flagged one-time-only, surfaced early (first
session or two) or at a planted moment-of-need (after a loss / a milestone).

## Tuning
- Price **$0.99-4.99**; the goal is conversion, not extraction (upsell later).
- **Over-deliver value** — first-purchase *satisfaction* drives the repeat rate, the real
  IAP engine.
- Anchor with a visible high "value" number; mark "one time only."
- Surface on the 2nd-3rd store visit if not bought on visit one (that's when most first
  buys land).

## Pitfalls
- Don't price so high it doesn't convert, nor so low it under-delivers and sets a weak
  anchor (DoF: most devs under-price — leave high-tier anchors in the catalog elsewhere).
- Must genuinely be one-time (Apple deceptive-metadata risk if "limited" offers recur as
  fake scarcity — see [[references/ethics-compliance-and-platform-rules.md]]).

## Live examples
Color Block Jam "Failed-Level Pack"; near-universal in top casual (2024-2026).

## Effort (solo dev)
Low-medium — one IAP product + an offer surface. Build after remove-ads. See
[[references/pricing-and-offers.md]].
