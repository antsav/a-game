# Go-To-Market & Marketing — organic-first, solo dev

How to market a **first** iOS game with **no/tiny budget.** You cannot win a paid UA war (funded
studios refresh 80–120 creatives/channel/month at iOS CPIs that swallow tiny budgets). Your edge is the
inverse: **one free organic clip can hit tens of thousands of impressions at zero marginal cost.** The
whole playbook is **organic-first, paid-as-amplifier-only.**

---

## 0. The marketability gate (a PRE-BUILD decision, not a launch task)

Before committing months of solo dev time, test whether the concept produces a **watchable, install-
driving 6-second clip** — because the same property that makes a good ad makes a good organic post. **If
the loop isn't legible and satisfying in ~3 seconds, organic-first growth is impossible regardless of
polish.** (This is also a `games-researcher` validation gate — coordinate with it.)

- **The 3-second rule:** a viewer must grasp what the game *feels* like and how it works within ~3
  seconds. Cut any beat that's confusing or non-additive in the opening.
- **Cheap solo test:** make 2–4 short vertical clips of the core loop (even from a rough prototype) and
  either post them organically and watch watch-time/comments, **or** spend a one-time **$50–150** on a
  single ad set to read CPI/IPM (targets in `games-researcher`). Clip performance = the marketability
  verdict.
- **Tailwind for this user:** a meditative, oddly-satisfying puzzle with burst-of-satisfaction moments
  is *inherently* clip-friendly — "satisfying/ASMR" is a top-performing organic genre **and** Apple-
  editorial-friendly. The taste north-star and organic distribution are aligned here.

## 1. Channels & the 2026 reach reality

**Blunt truth: TikTok organic reach collapsed.** Devlog/clip content that pulled millions in 2024 now
often averages **300–800 views**; one documented creator fell ~98.6% (425.8K → 6K) for the same content.
The "post a devlog, go viral" era is over. **Don't bet the launch on one platform.**

| Channel | Role | Notes (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| **TikTok (organic)** | Top discovery engine, but reach-gated | Treat as a daily lottery you enter |
| **Instagram Reels** | Co-primary — often now *outperforms* TikTok | Re-cut natively, don't repost with watermark |
| **YouTube Shorts** | Co-primary; best long-tail/searchability | Durable discovery vs TikTok's fast decay |
| **Reddit** | High-intent, niche | Honest "I made this" posts in genre subs; highest-converting word-of-mouth |
| **Discord** | Your owned "castle" | Early players → first sharers; survives algorithm changes |

**Content that drives installs (ranked for this user's taste):** oddly-satisfying/ASMR gameplay >
surprise/fail clips > trend-jacking (trending sounds) > complete-loop clips (full loop readable in <60s,
center-screen action).

**Cadence & expectations:** minimum **3–5 posts/week per platform**, aggressive **7–14 clips/week** across
all three. **The same clip can get 500 or 500,000 views** — hook and format decide, not the game alone.
Volume is the strategy: most clips die; you farm for the occasional breakout. Build a *habit*, not a
campaign.

## 2. Creatives for ~$0

**Anatomy of a winner:** hook in the **first 2–3 seconds** (lead with the payoff, not setup); legible in
3s; strong color contrast; natural gameplay speed; center-screen action; vertical 9:16. **UGC/authentic
("I found this game") beats polished trailers** — gloss reads as an ad and gets skipped. Show the
*feeling* (satisfaction/surprise), don't narrate rules. **Build virality hooks into the game itself**
(surprise outcomes, meme-able moments) — the design↔marketing link.

**How a solo dev makes them cheaply:** screen-record real gameplay, cut in **CapCut (free)**. Your
"creatives" and your "organic posts" are the *same assets* — no separate ad budget. Make many variants of
the **first 2 seconds** (different hooks, same loop) and let the platform tell you which hook wins.

---

## 3. Paid UA — mostly NOT for you (terrain + the one exception)

Know it so you recognize when it'd make sense (a publisher signs you, or a clip proves the concept).
iOS CPIs run ~2–3× Android; creative is the lever, not bid tuning. Networks: AppLovin/Unity/ironSource
(ad-monetized casual), Meta + Google App Campaigns (tier-1 volume), **Apple Search Ads** (the one cheap
paid lever worth a solo dev's attention — defend your own brand keyword + a few category terms).

**The one tactic that fits organic-first — "Paid Organic" amplification:** post a clip organically; if it
shows strong watch-time/engagement **in the first 24h**, put a small **~$200 boost (Spark Ad)** behind
*that proven winner only* to trigger broader algorithmic distribution. Track **cost-per-install, not
views. Never boost a clip that isn't already performing** — that's burning money.

## 4. ASO as a channel (brief — see `aso.md` for depth)

ASO is your free, always-on conversion layer and the bottom of every organic funnel — a viral clip is
wasted if the store page doesn't convert. The **first screenshot/preview video must pass the same 3-second
legibility test** as your clips. Full keyword/asset playbook in `aso.md`.

## 5. Community & pre-launch (start ~3 months out)

Marketing is **repetition, not an announcement** — the #1 failure is "nobody knew it existed." Start
audience-building **~3 months before launch** for a small mobile game (sustained, not a single drop).

**Owned channels (algorithm-proof "castle"):**
- **Landing page** capturing emails for a launch-notify list (offer something small — wallpaper, early
  TestFlight access).
- **Email list** — infrequent, value-packed; the asset you fully control.
- **Discord** — small server for earliest players/testers → your first sharers.
- **Build-in-public / devlogs** double as your daily clip pipeline.

**iOS specifics:** there's **no Steam-style wishlist.** Your pre-launch capture = **email list +
TestFlight beta sign-ups + Discord**, plus an iOS **pre-order** (converts to auto-downloads on release
day, concentrating launch-day velocity).

---

## 6. PR, featuring & influencers

- **Apple featuring is the single highest-leverage free lever** — file the nomination ≥3 weeks ahead with
  a *story* (not a version bump). Full mechanics in `launch-pipeline.md` Phase 4. A calm, design-forward
  puzzle is a gift for the editorial pitch.
- **Micro-influencers (10K–100K) > macro** — better ROI, more engaged, they actually play it. The 2026
  play is activating *many* micro-creators. Offer early access + codes; personalize; keep it brief.
- **Press:** pitch iOS outlets (TouchArcade-style) with a short personalized pitch + press kit (gifs, key
  art, one-line hook). One credible outlet covering you tends to get picked up by others.

## 7. The GTM sequence (concrete solo-dev playbook)

| Phase | When | Do this |
|---|---|---|
| **0. Marketability gate** | Before heavy build | 2–4 hook clips of the core loop. Post organically and/or $50–150 test. **No pass, no build.** |
| **1. Foundation** | T-3 months | Landing page + email capture; Discord; lock App Store name/keywords (`aso.md`); **start the 3–5×/week clip habit now.** |
| **2. Audience build** | T-3 to T-1 mo | Keep clipping across all 3 platforms; grow email + Discord; line up 20–50 micro-influencers; draft press kit. |
| **3. Apple nomination** | T-6 to T-2 weeks (≥2 wk min) | File featuring nomination ("App Launch") with the human/design story; open iOS **pre-order.** |
| **4. Beta** | T-4 to T-1 wk | TestFlight to Discord/email list; fix FTUE so store page + first session pass the 3-second test. |
| **5. Launch week** | Launch ±3 days | Concentrate: notify email list, post launch clips, send influencer codes + press pitches, mobilize Discord. Pre-orders auto-download = day-one velocity. |
| **6. Amplify** | Launch + days/weeks | "Paid Organic": ~$200 behind any clip already winning in 24h. Optional Apple Search Ads on brand keyword. |
| **7. Iterate** | Ongoing | Refresh store creatives/keywords from real data; in-game share prompts so players make clips; keep posting. |

**Weekly KPI discipline:** track cost/install + clip watch-time + comments; each week ask *"what's the
weakest number and the one action to fix it?"*

## 8. The mistakes first-timers make (avoid these)

1. **Marketing too late** (2 weeks out → 48h spike then flatline). 2. **Treating launch as one event**
not a pre/during/post sequence. 3. **"If it's good they'll come"** — nobody knowing is the #1 killer.
4. **Wrong channels** (not where players are). 5. **One rented platform** (TikTok-only) vs multi-platform
+ owned email/Discord. 6. **Boosting bad clips** (amplification only works on proven content).
7. **Scope creep** — turns a 3-month game into 18 months and starves marketing of time (**this user's
flagged risk — guard it**). 8. **Pitching Apple the version, not the story.**

**Realistic expectations:** organic reach is a volume game with mostly-dead posts; most first games don't
break out. The realistic win = a modest organic base + a few breakout clips + (ideally) an Apple feature,
compounded by a community that shares for you. Model success on **consistent posting + a tight launch week
+ one or two lucky clips you're positioned to catch** — not on 2024's viral fairy tales.
