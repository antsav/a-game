# Anton's Game Preference Profile

The durable source-of-truth for what Anton loves in a game. This is **him**, not
research — every design decision for the game should be checkable against this. When in
doubt about a feature, ask: *does this serve the profile, or fight it?*

> Last updated: 2026-06-29. This profile evolves — as Anton plays more and learns what he
> likes, update it here and log it in `CHANGELOG.md`. New observations land in `memory/`.

## The one-line north star

**Engagement through absorption, not arousal.** Anton wants a game that pulls you in
through calm focus, satisfying loops, and the intrinsic urge to reach for more — never
through stress, urgency, threat, or pressure. Meditative *and* engaging at the same time.

## What he loves (the signal)

- **Puzzle, single-player.** Solo, think-at-your-own-pace.
- **NO TIMER. Ever.** This is the single strongest, most-repeated signal. He explicitly
  only enjoys the no-timer versions of games he otherwise likes (e.g. untangle games). A
  timer is an instant disqualifier.
- **Meditative, relaxing — yet engaging.** The calm-but-absorbing sweet spot. Soothing,
  low-pressure, but with real pull. Not boring, not stressful.
- **Very simple start.** Low barrier to entry; learnable in seconds.
- **Difficulty that *cycles* — easy → moderate → hard and back.** A *rhythm* of tension
  and release, not a relentless ramp. Easy levels as breathers after hard ones.
- **Bursts of satisfaction and challenge *within* a level** — satisfying micro-payoffs
  (the solve, the cascade, the snap/pop), small challenges resolved with a satisfying click.
- **A "pull to reach more" — intrinsic, not pressured.** He loves idle games/clickers
  (Egg, Inc.) precisely because "there is no pressure, just a pull power to reach more."
  Motivation via anticipation, accumulation, and progress — **pull, never push.**

of idle games. He likes *calm optimization* and *satisfying resolution*, at his own pace.

## Hard rules (violating these breaks the profile)

- **No timers / countdowns / time-pressure** of any kind in core play.
- **No punishing fail states.** Failure should be soft — retry freely, no loss-anxiety.
- **No forced urgency / FOMO / pressure mechanics** (this constrains monetization — see
  the tension note below).
- **No relentless difficulty ramp.** Difficulty must oscillate; always provide breathers.
- **Pull, not push** — motivate via anticipation/progress/curiosity, never deadlines/loss.
- **Player controls the pace** — can pause/stop anytime, progress persists, optional goals.

## Strong preferences (serve these to go the extra mile)

- Meditative aesthetic: calm palette, soft audio/music, gentle particles, satisfying haptics.
- Heavy but *calm* "juice" — satisfying feedback that soothes rather than over-stimulates.
- An intrinsic progression pull (gentle numbers-go-up, collections, unlocks, "reach more").
- Simple one-touch / drag input; immediately readable feedback.
- An endless / Zen mode option fits his taste naturally.

## Known tension to manage (flag, don't ignore)

His "no pressure, no FOMO, no timers" taste **directly conflicts** with several standard
monetization levers (timed offers, energy gates, FOMO events — see `game-monetizer`). The
resolution: monetize via the *calm-compatible* tools — rewarded-ad acceleration, remove-ads,
cosmetic/relaxing-content IAP, gentle progression boosters — and avoid pressure-based
monetization that would betray the feel. When `game-monetizer` and this profile conflict,
**the feel wins for the core loop**; find a calm-compatible way to monetize around it.

## How this profile relates to the other skills
`games-researcher` finds a *marketable* concept; `game-monetizer` makes it *pay*; **this
profile makes sure it's something Anton will love enough to polish to the extra mile.** The
best concept sits at the intersection of all three — and happily, his taste (no-timer
arrow/sort puzzles, idle pull) overlaps with what's currently trending.
