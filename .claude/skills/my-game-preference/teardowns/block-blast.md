# Block Blast (block puzzle)

**Core loop:** Drag a tray of 3 random Tetris-like shapes onto an 8×8 grid; completing any
full row/column clears it; the round ends only when none of your current pieces fit.

**Source of satisfaction / pull:** Two layered hits — the micro-hit (the **line clear**) and
the macro-hit (the **combo/chain**: one placement clearing multiple lines for a multiplier,
"an addictive rush"). Rewards *forward planning* — holding board space for a clear you can see
coming. "A light IQ test without the pressure."

**Engaging without a timer:** The purest no-timer sandbox. No countdown, no objective bar —
pause mid-game anytime, lose nothing. Engagement engine = **failure-by-suffocation**: the
board slowly fills, and "no valid placement = game over" creates a quiet, self-paced tension
you fully control.

**Difficulty / pacing:** **Emergent, not authored** — no levels; difficulty arises as the board
fills and options shrink. Randomized pieces → "no two games the same," endless replay from
zero authored content. Low-stakes (short rounds, nothing long to lose) → experiment freely.

**DNA to extract:**
- **Endless self-paced board = infinite content from one well-built loop** (no 2,000-level
  treadmill — huge for a solo dev).
- **"Just one more"** from short rounds + score-chase + randomness (strongest retention pattern).
- **Layered satisfaction** — reliable small hit (line clear) + chased big hit (combo).
- **Suffocation as the fail-state** — pressure without a timer, player-controlled.

**Solo-dev buildability: VERY HIGH.** Grid logic, drag-drop, line-detection — all trivial. The
hard 20% is **juice** (snap, clear animation, combo escalation, sound) and **balance** (fair
piece-bag randomness). Note: market **saturated** with clones → a "better version" needs a real
differentiator (theme, a novel clear rule, daily challenges). Also note: Block Blast is *not*
purely "relaxing" — the self-paced board *is* the challenge. ~368M downloads in a year,
~$584K/day from ads alone (no IAP) — proof of the ads-first, no-pressure model.
