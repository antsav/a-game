# Block Out! Color Sort (color / ball / water sort)

**Core loop:** Several tubes hold stacked colored units in a jumble; move the top unit from one
tube to another only if colors match (or target is empty), aiming for each tube to hold a
single solid color.

**Source of satisfaction / pull:** The **"oddly satisfying" / completion-ASMR** genre. The pull
is *visual + auditory*: smooth pour animations, soft ASMR sounds, and the deep gratification of
a tube going jumbled → uniform. **Order from disorder at its most literal.** Each completed tube
is a discrete, tactile "click into place."

**Engaging without a timer:** Pure logical solve — "no timers and no move limits transforms what
could be a stressful logic puzzle into a meditative, relaxing experience." Undo, restart, stare
at it. Positioned as calming/brain-training, not competitive.

**Difficulty / pacing:** Discrete, guaranteed-solvable levels with **gentle constraint-tightening**
— more colors, more tubes, fewer free tubes (less maneuvering room). Challenge is constraint, not
speed.

**DNA to extract:**
- **The "oddly satisfying" sensory payoff IS the product** — animation + sound carry as much
  weight as the logic; the mechanic alone is dull, the juice makes it relaxing-yet-compelling.
- **Completion granularity** — many small finishes (each tube) per level = frequent micro-rewards.
- **Undo as a relaxation tool** — removing fear of mistakes is core to "calm."

**Solo-dev buildability: VERY HIGH (lowest-complexity logic of all).** Pure stack data structure
(each tube = a stack; legal move = top colors match). No grid, no physics (pour is faked
animation). The entire moat is **polish/feel** — also the hardest part without game-feel craft.
**Extremely saturated** → differentiation must come from theme/aesthetic/audio. **Best use: a
1-week warm-up project to build juice/feel muscles before a bigger bet.**
