# Planets Merge (2048-style merge)

*Clarification: Planets Merge is a **2048-style drag-merge grid game**, not a physics "Suika"
planet-drop game (Wildlife Studios, 4.4★, live Dec 2023 — older/lower-volume than the arrow titles).*

**Core loop:** Drag identical items together → they combine into the next tier (Moon→Mars→Earth→…
→Sun); each merge frees space and advances you up a visible chain of grander objects.

**Source of satisfaction / pull:** **Combining to *create*** — the reward is *upgrade/discovery*,
not destruction. Two small things become one bigger/better thing with a little burst. Collector's
pull (climbing the tier ladder, "what's the next planet?") + the gentle relief that **a merge
clears clutter** (two tiles → one). Merge-2 games add an unlock/collection meta — Liftoff 2025:
merge-2 hits **D30 retention >25%** (higher than many casual genres), driven by "clear progress
and regular rewards."

**Engaging without a timer:** Self-paced; the only pressure is **spatial** (board fills; overflow
= game over — same suffocation model as Block Blast). Progression/collection goals provide the
forward pull, not urgency.

**Difficulty / pacing:** **Very gentle** — early merges trivial and constant (fast frequent
rewards), difficulty creeps as higher tiers need exponentially more inputs and space tightens.
Among the softest ramps of any puzzle genre → beginner- and casual-friendly.

**DNA to extract:**
- **Creation/upgrade as the reward** (vs clearing/destroying) — a warmer, more "progress-y"
  feeling; strong for relaxation + long-term retention.
- **Visible tier ladder** = built-in "what's next?" pull and collection drive.
- **Soft suffocation fail-state** (board fills) = stakes with no clock.
- **Merge-2 meta layer** (unlocks, areas, gentle goals) is a retention multiplier — *but the
  scope-creep trap.*

**Solo-dev buildability: MEDIUM.** The **2048-grid merge core is HIGH** (grid, drag, merge-equal
logic — near as simple as ball-sort). But a *full Merge-2* game (boards, generators, energy,
quests, story) is the **most scope-heavy genre in the library.** **Lesson: steal the merge *core*
(simple, satisfying), avoid the merge-2 *meta* (sprawling)** for a first ship.

**Sources:** Planets Merge Google Play / App Store (Wildlife Studios) · Playablemaker "Understanding
Merge2" · Liftoff 2025 Casual Report. Accessed Jun 2026.
