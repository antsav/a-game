# Genre DNA — The Fingerprint of Anton's Taste

The synthesis across the games Anton loves: what the families have in common, the bridge
that links his idle love to his puzzle love, and which sub-genres best fit a calm,
no-timer, solo-buildable iOS game. Per-game deep dives live in `../teardowns/`.

## The common DNA (this IS the taste profile, distilled)

Across arrow-untangle, block, sort, match-3, merge, idle, tower defense, and turn-based
strategy, the same fingerprint repeats:

1. **No clock, but stakes exist — "stakes without speed."** Not one uses a real-time
   countdown as core tension, yet most have a *fail-state*: collision (Arrows), suffocation
   (Block Blast, merge), move-limit (Candy Crush), soft retry (TD/Strategy). **A consequence
   the player controls the pace of approaching.** This is the #1 piece of DNA — it's what
   makes them *relaxing-yet-engaging* rather than merely relaxing.
2. **Order from disorder.** Every one is emotionally *tidying up*: untangle, clear lines,
   sort colors, match, consolidate merges, fill a map. A messy board becoming clean = the
   satisfaction engine. (Restoring order is a primal human pleasure.)
3. **One small action → a visible, slightly-larger reward.** A tap clears a lane; a placement
   clears two lines; a swap cascades; a merge tiers up. Input small, payoff a satisfying
   *little* eruption. Never grindy, never one-to-one.
4. **Trivial to learn, no tutorial/story needed.** Wordless, instantly legible, glanceable.
5. **Self-paced sessions of any length.** 20 seconds or 20 minutes; pausable mid-thought.
6. **Clean, minimal, "satisfying" sensory layer.** Stark visuals + tactile feedback + (sort/
   merge) ASMR audio. The *feel* is the product as much as the logic.

## The PULL / NO-PRESSURE split (from the idle/TD/strategy cluster)

**Keep all of the PULL:**
- A number/board state that **always goes up, never comes down** (the dopamine engine).
- An **always-visible next goal** (next unlock + its price) — anticipation with zero loss-risk.
- A **persistent meta-layer that survives resets** (Egg Inc. Soul Eggs) → resetting =
  *banking power*, never losing.
- **Multi-horizon goals** — a 5-second, 5-day, and 5-week carrot at once.
- **Optimization as the engagement substrate** — the brain stays busy solving a small puzzle
  (which upgrade / where to place). *This is the bridge to puzzle taste.*

**Remove all of the PUSH:**
- No move/wave/placement **timers** — *the player presses the button that advances time*
  (Dampf's player-started waves; Strategery's end-turn-when-you-like). The single most
  transferable "calm switch."
- No **punishing fail-state** — soft retries that keep meta-progress; difficulty scales *with*
  the player, never in spikes.
- No **energy gates / forced ads** — non-aggressive, opt-in monetization is the repeatedly
  cited *trust moat*.
- **Offline/idle progress** — being away is a *reward*, not a penalty.
- **Cozy, legible aesthetic** — calm visuals, transparent rules.

## The bridge insight (idle ⟷ puzzle)

Idle's true engine isn't tapping — it's **the satisfaction of an optimization system that
visibly grows.** A puzzle is *also* an optimization act with a visible "solved" payoff. So the
two fuse cleanly: **make the puzzle the *generator* in an idle loop.** Concretely:
- **Puzzle solves = the currency tap.** Each solved board emits a resource; good solves/combos
  pay more (the optimization pull).
- **Spend on upgrades that make future puzzles more *rewarding*** (multipliers, helpers that
  auto-clear simple tiles, extra moves) — growth, not gating; never *harder*.
- **Offline/idle generation** so the meta ticks up between sessions → opening the app is always
  a reward → voluntary return without nag-notifications.
- **A prestige layer** — reset the puzzle-economy for a permanent multiplier and a fresh,
  faster climb. (This *also* solves number-balancing — prestige reins in exponential curves.)
- **A visibly-growing board/world** (builder DNA) — solved puzzles physically build/expand
  something (territory, island, garden), giving progression a *tangible body*, not just a counter.
- **Player-paced everything** — no puzzle timer, no punishing move-limit; difficulty scales
  with the meta-multipliers so it never spikes.

This fusion is the most direct expression of Anton's whole taste in one design.

## Which sub-genres fit best (ranked for Anton: no craft yet, ship-fast, ride a real trend)

1. **🥇 Arrow / line-untangling (Amaze GO! type) — best overall bet now.** The *current
   breakout trend* (May 2026, 50M+ combined downloads, multiple publishers = validated demand,
   still early), the genre he *most recently loved* (intuition + motivation), and the best
   buildability-to-trend ratio: dead-simple logic (raycast collision, no physics/art/feel
   mastery), levels **procedurally generate-and-verify** (no 2,000-level treadmill). "Stakes
   without a clock" built in. *Watch-outs:* competition arriving fast → ship soon, differentiate
   (better level-gen, a novel arrow rule, daily puzzles); pure-logic puzzles monetize weakly on
   IAP → plan ads-first. **Cross-skill note:** this is the same arrow-puzzle wave `games-researcher`
   flagged as the freshest breakout — taste and market coincide here.
2. **🥈 Block puzzle (Block Blast type) — best retention, zero level-authoring.** Endless
   self-paced board = infinite content from one loop. Strongest "just one more." Trivial logic.
   *Watch-outs:* saturated → needs a real differentiator; the win/loss is *all in the juice*
   (the skill not yet built).
3. **🥉 Color sort (Block Out! type) — easiest to build, hardest to stand out.** Lowest-
   complexity logic (just stacks). Pure calm. Great as a **1-week warm-up to learn game-feel**
   before a bigger bet. *Watch-out:* commodity mechanic, brutally crowded — moat is polish/theme/audio.
- **Merge core (2048-grid):** buildable, warm/satisfying, decent retention — but trend is older
  (2023) and the temptation to balloon into a full merge-2 meta is a scope trap. Lower priority.
- **Idle/incremental:** *highest* buildability of all (one dev, the cost is balancing not tech);
  the purest "pull." Best layered *into* a puzzle (the bridge above) rather than shipped raw.
- **Tower defense / turn-based strategy:** medium buildability (well-trodden Unity); calm only
  if you flip the switches (player-pressed wave/turn, soft fail, no energy). Mine for the
  *player-paced-advance* switch.
- **❌ Match-3 (Candy Crush): do NOT pick first.** Heaviest content + balancing burden (hand-
  designed levels, special-piece matrix), market owned by giants. Mine for *ideas* (cascades,
  move-limit-not-time-limit), don't build as game #1.

**Bottom line:** the arrow/untangle genre is the rare alignment of *a trend Anton personally
loves*, *moderate-but-rising competition*, *validated demand*, and *the lowest craft barrier of
anything he'd be excited to build* — exactly the "better version of an emerging trend" thesis.
Optionally fuse in a gentle idle/prestige progression + a visibly-growing board to nail the
"pull to reach more." Consider a small color-sort first as a feel-building warm-up.
